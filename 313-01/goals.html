MATH 313-01: Course Goals
Instructor: Misha Shvartsman 

General Goals:
1. Gaining factual knowledge (terminology and methods)
2. Learning to apply course material (problem solving)

Subject Goals:

1. To learn the main ideas and concepts of probability theory, to calculate probabilities using the mathematical definition of probability and combinatorial methods
2. To understand and use the concept of conditional probability, to identify independent events, to be able to use tree diagrams and the Bayes theorem to evaluate conditional probabilities
3. To recognize the nature of random variables and vectors, describe random variables by their distributions, and work with univariate and multivariate distributions
4. To describe random variables with the help of expectation and variance
5. To be able to the concepts of joint distributions and transformations of random variables
6. To understand and use the concept of conditional expectation 
7. To be able to formulate and apply the Law of Large Numbers and the Central Limit Theorem
