MATH 316-01:  Course Goals
Instructor: Misha Shvartsman 

General Goals:
1. Gaining factual knowledge (terminology and methods)
2. Learning to apply course material (problem solving)

Subject Goals: 
1. To gain factual knowledge about how to use applied mathematics in solving problems of applied science.
2. To learn principles and methods of applied mathematics including some skills in proving relevant theorems.
3. In some of the assignments you will be asked to write a simple code to illustrate a specific method of solving
a problem or illustrtate the behavior of its solution. Preferred tool is Mathematica but you can use any other
package and/or programming language.
4. Develop skills in expressing mathematical and scientific work orally and in writing. Instead of a final exam you
will be asked to complete a final project. Two requirements for this assignment will be to write a project report
and to present your work to the class.
